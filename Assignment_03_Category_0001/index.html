Frequently Asked Questions

What should I consider before adopting a pet?

Before adopting a pet, consider your lifestyle, living space, time availability, and financial commitment. Make sure you can provide proper care, nutrition, exercise, and veterinary attention for your new companion.

How do I choose the right pet for my family?

Consider your family's activity level, space, and experience with pets. Research different breeds and their temperaments. Visit shelters to meet pets in person and consult with adoption counselors who can help match you with the perfect companion.

What are the basic supplies I need for a new pet?

Essential supplies include food and water bowls, appropriate food, a comfortable bed, toys, grooming tools, collar and leash (for dogs), litter box (for cats), and a carrier for vet visits. Don't forget to schedule a veterinary checkup soon after adoption.

How often should I take my pet to the vet?

Puppies and kittens need several vet visits in their first year for vaccinations and checkups. Adult pets should have annual wellness exams, while senior pets may need checkups every 6 months. Always consult your vet if you notice any health concerns.

What is the adoption process like?

The adoption process typically includes filling out an application, meeting with the pet, a home visit or interview, and paying an adoption fee. This process helps ensure the pet goes to a suitable, loving home where they'll thrive.

Can I return a pet if it doesn't work out?

Most shelters and rescues have return policies, though they vary by organization. We encourage adopters to give pets time to adjust (usually 2-3 weeks) and reach out for support if challenges arise. Our team is here to help ensure successful adoptions.
